Annual Events

Throughout the year, Renge-in Temple hosts various traditional Buddhist ceremonies and cultural events. These events mark important dates in the Buddhist calendar and provide opportunities for spiritual practice, community gathering, and cultural celebration.

We welcome visitors to join us for these special occasions. Please contact the temple office for specific times and details about upcoming events.

Spring Events

Spring Equinox Service (Higan-e)

March 18-24

A week-long observance centered around the spring equinox, dedicated to remembering and honoring ancestors. Daily services are held in the Main Hall during this period.

Buddha's Birthday (Hanamatsuri)

April 8

A joyful celebration of the birth of Shakyamuni Buddha. The ceremony includes the pouring of sweet tea (amacha) over a small statue of the baby Buddha, symbolizing the sweet rain that fell during his birth.

Summer Events

Obon Festival

August 13-16

A traditional Buddhist event to honor the spirits of ancestors. During this period, spirits are believed to return to this world to visit their relatives. Special services are held daily in the Main Hall.

Segaki Service

August 15

A special ceremony held during Obon to remember and honor those who have passed away without family to remember them. This compassionate service helps ease the suffering of hungry ghosts and brings peace to wandering spirits.

Autumn Events

Autumn Equinox Service (Higan-e)

September 20-26

Similar to the Spring Equinox Service, this week-long observance is centered around the autumn equinox and is dedicated to remembering and honoring ancestors.

Juya Service

October 15

A special service to praise the vows of Amida Buddha. This ceremony focuses on the compassionate vows of Amida Buddha to save all sentient beings.

Winter Events

New Year's Eve Bell Ringing (Joya no Kane)

December 31

A traditional ceremony where the temple bell is rung 108 times at midnight to symbolize the cleansing of the 108 earthly desires in Buddhist belief. This ritual helps to purify the mind and soul before entering the new year.

New Year's Service (Shusho-e)

January 1-3

Special services held during the first three days of the new year to pray for peace, health, and prosperity in the coming year. Visitors can receive blessings and special amulets for the new year.
